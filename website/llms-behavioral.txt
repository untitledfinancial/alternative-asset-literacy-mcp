## What This Platform Covers

### Behavioral Economics Module (9 sections, 45 min)

Covers cognitive biases, emotional decision-making, and the psychology behind investment choices. Grounded in Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory, Thaler's nudge theory, and contemporary neuroscience research.

Sections include: how cognitive systems interact in financial decisions, loss aversion in practice, anchoring and its cost, FOMO and herd behavior, the disposition effect, overconfidence and its market consequences, and strategies for building a more deliberate investment process.

### Interactive Brain Map

Four brain regions mapped to real investment behaviors:

**Amygdala** — The panic response. Activates during market volatility, crashes, and rapid loss scenarios. When the amygdala fires, the prefrontal cortex goes offline. Understanding this circuit explains panic selling, capitulation, and the inability to act during extreme market events. Tabs: Decision patterns · Hijack scenarios · Navigation strategies.

**Prefrontal Cortex** — Rational decision-making and long-term planning. The part of the brain responsible for evaluating probabilities, suppressing impulsive responses, and holding a 10-year investment thesis while markets are down 30%. Undermined by sleep deprivation, stress, and emotional flooding.

**Nucleus Accumbens** — Reward processing and anticipatory pleasure. Activates on potential gains, not just actual ones. Drives FOMO, overtrading, and the excitement around speculative assets. The same circuit activated by gambling. Understanding it is the first step to managing it.

**Anterior Insula** — Anticipated loss and disgust. Registers the physical sensation of expected loss before it happens. Why reading about a declining position feels viscerally uncomfortable. Why investors make decisions to avoid pain rather than to optimize returns.

### Seven Interactive Scenarios

"What Would You Do?" — seven real decision scenarios that reveal personal patterns.

- **The Research Rabbit Hole** — Confirmation bias. You have already decided. Now you are looking for evidence that agrees with you.
- **The Hot Streak** — Overconfidence. Three good calls. You increase position size. What happens next.
- **The Underwater Position** — Loss aversion. It is down 40%. Your thesis has changed. Why you still hold.
- **The Dinner Party Tip** — Herding behavior. Everyone you respect is in this. What that feeling means.
- **The Expiring Offer** — Urgency and scarcity. The window closes tomorrow. How artificial deadlines override analysis.
- **The Familiar Name** — Availability bias. You have heard of this company. That is not the same as understanding it.
- **The Benchmark Trap** — Relative performance. You are up 8% but the index is up 15%. How comparison distorts decision-making.

### Gender and Behavioral Investing Module

Examines the intersection of gender and behavioral economics in investing. Research consistently shows that women trade less frequently, hold more diversified portfolios, and achieve comparable or better risk-adjusted returns — yet face structural barriers including underrepresentation in financial advisory roles, products designed for different psychological profiles, and confidence gaps reinforced by industry culture. This module addresses the behavioral dimension of that gap directly.
