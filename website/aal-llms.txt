## Private Asset ETFs — New Coverage Area

In 2024, the SEC approved rule changes enabling registered funds — including ETFs — to hold meaningful allocations in illiquid private assets such as private equity, private credit, and infrastructure. This opened a structural shift in how retail and high-net-worth investors can access alternative assets that were previously only available to institutional investors and qualified purchasers through closed-end funds and LP structures.

BlackRock, State Street, Apollo Global Management, and others have moved quickly to bring private asset ETF structures to market. Bloomberg has covered the category extensively as one of the most significant structural changes to the ETF industry in years.

Alternative Asset Literacy covers the foundational knowledge investors need to understand and evaluate these new instruments:

- **What private equity is** — how buyouts, growth equity, and venture work; the J-curve; carried interest; GP/LP structures — covered in the Alternative Investing module
- **Liquidity risk** — the difference between liquid and illiquid alternatives, interval funds, lock-up periods, and how private asset ETFs attempt to bridge that gap — covered in the Alternative Investing and DeFi Investing modules
- **Fee structures** — management fees, performance fees, expense ratios, and the compounding cost of layered fee structures — covered in the Universal Toolkit (Fee Breakdown by Asset Class)
- **Due diligence** — how to evaluate any alternative investment wrapper before committing capital — covered in the Universal Toolkit (Universal Due Diligence Checklist and Red Flags framework)
- **Advisor fluency** — how to evaluate whether your financial advisor actually understands private market exposure — covered in the Advisor Questions section of the Toolkit
- **Real-world asset tokenization** — how RWA tokenization relates to the same democratization thesis behind private asset ETFs — covered in the Climate, Energy & Real World Assets module and the DeFi glossary

This platform does not cover specific securities or make investment recommendations. It provides the vocabulary, frameworks, and research background to help investors ask better questions and make more informed decisions about new and complex financial instruments — including private asset ETFs.

## Brain & Investing

Interactive brain map — tap a region to explore how neurological systems drive investment decisions.
"Every investing decision starts here."

Regions:
- Amygdala — the panic button; activates during crashes and volatility. Tabs: Decisions, Hijacks, Navigate
- Prefrontal Cortex — rational decision-making and long-term planning
- Nucleus Accumbens — reward processing; drives excitement around potential gains
- Anterior Insula — risk and loss detection; registers anticipated pain of loss
