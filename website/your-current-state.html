Your Current State_

Writer Catherine Lacey sums it up well: "Women so often carry this delusion of being innately wrong, of needing to be numbed, controlled, caged or hidden — a delusion smuggled into us in the Trojan horse of a thousand old stories." In the context of our current social structures, one may feel the weight of personal responsibility outweighs the relevance of systematic biases. While it is important to live in a place of radical responsibility, it would be an injustice not to name those delusions clearly.

How You Got Here_

There are systems of oppression, injustice, marginalization, and institutional neglect outside of your control. You deserve access to financial instruments that allow for financial safety, freedom, and most of all respect.

Aim, if possible, to let go of the mindset that there is only one specific process for investing. There are multiple avenues to the same goal — financial freedom and long-term wealth being the journey.

You may have the finances to invest from your income, other portfolio contributions, alimony, divorce, or an inheritance. Many women step into wealth through the unfortunate event of becoming a widow. Other women have access to family money built through the power and fortitude of generations past.

However you arrived at this path — it is your path. And your birthright.

The Numbers Behind the Story_

Many have been in the workforce since their teens — navigating corporate life and structures that make it difficult to naturally grow their wealth when earning $0.80 on the dollar.

Several have gone down the route of entrepreneurship. As of 2019:

  • 42% of all U.S. businesses and firms were owned by women
  • 1 in 5 firms with revenue of $1M or more is female-owned

"The potential of women entrepreneurs for spurring economic growth has not been fully realized."
— State of Women-Owned Business Report, American Express (2019)

Bloomberg shed light on a survey finding that almost half of 2021's new entrepreneurs were women — a 28% increase from 2019.

Many are now in the position to make true dividends from their hard work, income streams, and asset allocations. The capital is there. The question is where it goes next.

The Financing Gap_

Globally, there is a $1.7 trillion financing gap for women-owned businesses — the difference between what women-owned enterprises need in capital and what they currently receive.

That gap is not a reflection of risk. It is a reflection of who has historically controlled the allocation of capital.

Technology and regulatory updates have begun to change the infrastructure. Alternative assets — particularly DeFi, ESG vehicles, and newer private market structures — represent part of what fills that gap. Not as charity. As returns.

McKinsey on What Needs to Change_

A McKinsey study called for specific interventions to address attitudinal biases in financial services. The finding is not that women are less capable investors. The finding is that the systems evaluating them carry embedded assumptions that do not hold up under scrutiny.

The last thing women need is the burden of institutional neglect layered onto the already complex work of long-term wealth building.

We know that. That is why this platform exists.
